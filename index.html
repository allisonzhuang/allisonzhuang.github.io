Hello!

I’m Allison Zhuang, a rising sophomore at Dartmouth College. I love puns, the smell of rain, and creating things that delight and inspire.

One of 8 Jack Byrne Scholars in my year, I’m currently pursuing a double major in mathematics and computer science. Math forms the foundation for all computational methods and the runway for future innovations, like number theory in cybersecurity and linear algebra in machine learning. Meanwhile, computer science is a powerful mechanism for truth-seeking in mathematics, be it by simulating probabilities or solving equations where algebraic tricks fall short.

For fun, I’m training to become a Trip Leader for the DOC and First-Year Trips, and I sing for the Glee Club and am a member of the Association for Women in Mathematics Executive Board. I’m also an avid foodie and traveler. In my free time, I fold origami, write poetry and short stories, break the fourth wall, and take photos–you can see some if you keep scrolling :)

PROJECTS

Things I've created!


Networks Robustness Library

As a WiSP intern, I co-wrote a coding library to generate scale-free and random networks, simulate attacks and failures, and plot the networks' performance over a variety of metrics: efficiency, largest cluster size, and average cluster size. Applications range from electronics to social networks to cell biology.

Project Euler Repository

There’s nothing better than cracking a hard problem with a team of good friends. I’m a contributor to a Github repository of solutions to Project Euler problems, the internet’s most popular trove of challenges in mathematical computing.

Impossible without raw computing power, and impossibly slow by brute force, Euler problems marry the best of computer science and mathematics. Coded in C++ to practice a fast, low-level programming language, I’ve written solutions to problems 1, 6, 69, and 99 that work in microseconds.

Useful Proof 2

My most revolutionary work of original mathematical scholarship to date, Useful Proof 2 can nonetheless be understood and enjoyed by anyone with a high school-level math education. Read it! :)
